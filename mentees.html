PAST MENTEES

2019-2020

Silvana Delgado

she / her / hers

I was born and raised in Ecuador, but I’ve also lived in a bunch of states in the USA. I am constantly craving travel, the outdoors, & culturally diverse environments. I enjoy learning new skills (and languages) and also love climbing, playing ukulele, being with loved ones, etc.! Some topics I am passionate about: the environment, social justice, animal rights, & diversity & inclusion in STEM (outreach).

I am a 2nd year grad student and I work with Jonelle Walsh. Currently, I am working with data from the Mitchell IFS (McDonald Observatory) and GMOS IFS (Gemini Observatory) to do dynamical mass modeling of nearby massive early-type galaxies. I am really interested in galaxy formation and evolution, and studying the central black hole in nearby galaxies is a great way to understand different types of galaxies and how they evolve with their SMBH through cosmic time. In my undergrad, I was a part of a few research projects studying high-z submillimeter galaxies, H-alpha variability of young low-mass stars, and satellite galaxies of Milky Way analogs (with Louie!).

Addy Evans

she / her / hers

I’m originally from Oklahoma and my ultimate goal is to convince all of my friends to buy a plot of land together so we can build houses next to each other and share a garden (hmu if you’re interested). When I’m not debugging code or thinking about the cosmos, I enjoy creating art, primarily through poetry, drawing, and graphic design but I enjoy all kinds of self-expression! I try to seek out inspiration wherever & whenever I can and I love spending time with my friends. Sometimes I get tired though and I spend the weekend watching movies instead.

I’m a second year graduate student working with Louis Strigari in the Milky Way Group. I have several research interests but my main pursuit is seeking to understand more about the particle nature of dark matter and its role in cosmic-scale structures. To do this, I study a combination of the stellar kinematics of galactic objects as well as the radiation coming from these systems. I’m also interested in ultra-high energy cosmic rays and neutrinos. Basically I’m a fan of radiation physics.

Xingzhuo Chen (Gesa)

he / him / his

I came from a port city & also library city in China, and finished my undergraduate in the spiciest city in China. Apart from science, I like to write science fiction, go bike riding, & enjoy the sunlight. As a MAGIC mentee, I was guided by Yaswant, Peter and Vince in my first year at TAMU. We chatted a lot about science and life in College Station, and it is really uplifting when we are working together on class tasks.

Currently, I am a second year graduate student. My interest is simulating supernova (SN) explosions using supercomputers, applying deep-learning onto various kinds of astronomical data, & any other big-and-messy problems between astronomy and physics. I have been working with Lifan Wang for two years and we have tried to find SNe from DECam’s multi-band image data; measuring SN element abundances from HST spectra; & studying SN host galaxy properties using IFU spectrograph from MUSE, MaNGA & Califa.

Micalyn Rowe

I am a born and raised Texan, and I am half Filipina and half white. I have a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Physics with a minor in Astronomy from Texas A&M University-Commerce. I love to read books, spend time outdoors with Heath (human partner) and Beans (doggie), watch interesting TV, photograph special moments, travel, and expand my horizons.
